## Strategic Summary

### The Core Bet

Nigerian fashion retail is the wedge. WhatsApp is the delivery mechanism. AI is the differentiator. The goal is not to be the best WhatsApp tool - it is to become the operating system for Nigerian fashion SME commerce.

The defensible position is not the bot. It is:
1. **Merchant relationship density** - deep integration into how they run their business
2. **Catalog and order data** - proprietary dataset of Nigerian fashion product inventory and buyer behavior
3. **Payment rails** - becoming the trusted layer between merchant and Paystack/Flutterwave
4. **AI trained on Nigerian context** - pidgin, product terminology, pricing norms, buyer objections

### The 90-Day Priority Decisions

| Decision | Stakes | Current Lean |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Build vs. buy catalog management | Build-now enables moat; buy-now is faster | Build |
| Hire sales first or engineer first | Sales validates faster; engineering enables scale | Hire 1 engineer + 1 sales simultaneously |
| Lagos-only or national launch | Lagos has density; national disperses effort | Lagos only for 6 months |
| Free trial or paid-from-day-1 | Trial reduces friction; paid filters for serious merchants | 14-day trial |
| WhatsApp-only or multi-channel | WhatsApp is Nigeria; SMS/email adds complexity | WhatsApp-only for 12 months |

### Exit Thesis

**Most likely exit:** Acquisition by a Nigerian or pan-African fintech/commerce platform (Flutterwave, Paystack/Stripe, Jumia, or WATI parent company).

**Acquisition trigger:** When Lameda reaches 2,000-5,000 merchants with verifiable GMV flowing through the payment rails, the platform becomes a distribution asset for any company wanting SME access in Nigeria.

**Target exit timeline:** Series A at Month 18, strategic acquisition at Year 3-4.

**Alternative path:** If WhatsApp commerce grows 10x across Africa, Lameda could raise a Series B and expand to Ghana, Kenya, South Africa independently.

### Moat Analysis

| Moat Type | Current Strength | 12-Month Strength |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Data (catalog + orders) | Weak (no merchants yet) | Medium (500 merchants = signal) |
| Network effects | None | Weak (referral program) |
| Switching costs | Low | Medium (catalog + conversation history locked in) |
| Brand | None | Weak-medium in Lagos fashion circles |
| Technical | Medium (AI + vector search) | Medium (competitors can copy) |
| Regulatory | None | Low (NDPR compliance advantage) |

**Honest assessment:** Moat is thin in Year 1. Speed and merchant relationships are the actual advantage in the short term. The data moat only materializes at 1,000+ merchants.

### Contrarian Views

1. **"WhatsApp will ban commerce bots"** - Unlikely. Meta is actively investing in WhatsApp commerce globally. Nigeria is a priority market. The risk is policy tightening, not elimination.

2. **"Nigerian SMEs won't pay for software"** - Wrong framing. Nigerian SMEs pay for things that directly make them money. Lameda is sold as an employee (N10K/month) not a tool. Comparison: a physical shop assistant costs N80K-150K/month.

3. **"The AI will make mistakes and destroy trust"** - True risk but manageable. The solution is not to make the AI perfect - it is to make errors recoverable and visible. Merchant override + confidence thresholds + human escalation path.

4. **"WATI or ManyChat will add AI and win"** - They will add AI. They will not understand Nigerian fashion. The moat is vertical depth, not AI capability alone.

## Strategic Milestones and Decision Gates

### Gate 1 (Month 3): Product-Market Fit Signal
- 15 paying merchants
- 80% trial-to-paid conversion
- Under 5% monthly churn
- At least 3 unsolicited referrals

**If not met:** Investigate pricing, onboarding friction, or product fit before scaling sales.

### Gate 2 (Month 6): Growth Engine Validated
- 50 merchants
- Net Revenue Retention above 100% (expansion revenue)
- Unit economics confirmed (CAC payback under 6 months)

**If not met:** Pause paid acquisition; focus on referral-only growth until metrics improve.

### Gate 3 (Month 12): Series A Readiness
- 300+ merchants
- N4.5M+ MRR
- Clear path to N100M ARR
- Operational team in place (not founder-dependent)

**If not met:** Extend seed runway (bridge round) or pivot thesis.

### Gate 4 (Month 18): Series A Milestone
- 500 merchants
- N7.5M MRR
- N90M ARR run rate
- 2 additional Nigerian cities beyond Lagos

**If met:** Raise Series A (N500M-N1B target) to expand to Ghana and Kenya.
