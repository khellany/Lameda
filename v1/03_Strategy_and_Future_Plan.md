# Strategy and Future Product Plan

## Positioning
Lameda is a conversational commerce operating layer for SMEs. It is not positioned as a generic chatbot. It is positioned as a revenue and support system inside the channels customers already use.

## Beachhead Market
The first beachhead is small Nigerian fashion retailers with:
- under 100 products,
- 20 or more daily conversations,
- order values above ₦50,000,
- and WhatsApp as the primary sales channel.

## Why This Market First
- Strong pain.
- Clear revenue leakage.
- Easy to explain value.
- Faster pilot access.
- Straightforward product catalog structure.

## Commercial Strategy
1. Validate on 3 to 5 pilot retailers.
2. Charge early after trial.
3. Prove ROI through recovered sales and reduced manual work.
4. Expand to adjacent verticals once onboarding is repeatable.

## Go-To-Market Strategy
- Founder-led sales and demos.
- Merchant communities and referral loops.
- Social proof from pilot users.
- Short demo videos showing product discovery and payment.
- Lightweight onboarding promise: live in under 30 minutes.

## Monetization Strategy
- Monthly SaaS subscription.
- Discounted annual billing.
- Optional premium support and setup.
- Later: usage-based messaging or transaction-based upsells.

## Product Growth Strategy
### Phase 1
- Product catalog.
- Chat search.
- Order capture.
- Payment.
- Human handoff.

### Phase 2
- Segmentation.
- Broadcast campaigns.
- Testimonials.
- Reorder nudges.
- Better analytics.

### Phase 3
- Deeper delivery integrations.
- Multi-agent support.
- Rule automation builder.
- Rich CRM capabilities.

### Phase 4
- Regional expansion.
- Multi-vertical templates.
- Ecosystem partnerships.
- Acquisition readiness.

## Defensibility
- Channel-specific workflow data.
- Merchant switching costs.
- Local payment and support depth.
- Product experience tuned to real merchant pain.

## Long-Term Vision
Lameda can evolve into a full customer operating system for chat-based commerce and potentially into a regional commerce infrastructure layer. The long-term acquisition thesis is credible because the product sits close to channel behavior, transaction data, and merchant engagement.

## Major Strategic Risks
- Platform policy dependence.
- Onboarding friction.
- Lack of customer trust in automation.
- Complexity creep.

## Strategic Principle
Build the smallest product that consistently converts chat into payment and reduce manual work. Expand only after that loop is proven.
